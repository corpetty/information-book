## Thesis

Virality is inversely related to complexity. Big networks can only hold simple, emotionally-charged "truths" because every hop compresses, distorts, or drops nuance. The complex form survives only where institutional carriers (Harari's bureaucracy) pre-load preconditions on receivers. Most current information technology expands the network without expanding the receiver budget, so the complexity ceiling drops and what's left fits the medium: weaponizable emotional payload. The way through is plural: multiple smaller networks each holding higher-resolution ideas, with infrastructure that protects integrity across the gaps.

## Structure

### Part I. The Pipeline

How information moves from reality to people.

**Chapter 1: The Information Landscape.** The pipeline from The Out There → Raw Data → Insight → Theory → News → Meme. Each arrow is a transformation. Drafted as [[the-information-landscape]] with a graphviz diagram. Next pass: turn each arrow into its own section (Measurement, Analysis, Consensus, Curation, Emote) and describe what each transformation does and at what cost.

**Chapter 2: What Gets Lost At Each Step.** Skeleton. Characterize the lossy compression at each stage of the pipeline. Each transformation is necessary for the next layer to work and lossy by construction. Probably the right home for Harari's three-realities framework (objective / subjective / intersubjective) from [[nexus-book]].

### Part II. Why It Has To Be Lossy

The structural reasons compression isn't avoidable.

**Chapter 3: The Human Time Budget.** Tablespoon of weeks, lens shaped by trauma and culture, the asymmetry between nominal and effective mental hours. Drafted as [[info-time-limit]] but only the first half is done. Need to write the "How much information can we take in?" section.

**Chapter 4: Optionality vs. Access.** The general-vs-usable trade-off. Drafted as [[optionality vs access]] using matplotlib/seaborn. Needs generalization beyond software: religions, political platforms, scientific popularization, legal codes. Same curve everywhere.

**Chapter 5: The Complexity / Virality Trade-off.** *Load-bearing.* Drafted as [[complexity-virality-tradeoff]] with Harari worked in. Currently in workshop. Refinements likely after feedback.

### Part III. The Bridge Zone

Where complex specialist knowledge gets transformed (and often destroyed) on its way to mass audiences.

**Chapter 6: Where It All Gets Fucked Up.** Skeleton. The space between deep specialists and mass audiences. Journalism, popularizers, influencers, AI summarizers. Who lives in this zone, what incentives shape what they do, examples of catastrophic distortion (COVID epidemiology, nutrition science, monetary policy popularization).

**Chapter 7: Emotional Memetics As The Floor.** Skeleton. Once the network is big enough, the prevailing ideas are whatever evokes raw strong emotions. Argument for why this is the stable equilibrium under the trade-off, not a contingent failure of any particular platform.

### Part IV. A Way Through

Hypothesis territory. Least-finished part of the book.

**Chapter 8: Multiple Small Networks > One Big One.** Skeleton. The argument from [[general-theme]] expanded. Plural participation in smaller groups, each holding complex value sets. How groups self-form, change, drift, and why this beats centralized aggregation on robustness.

**Chapter 9: What Current Information Tech Actively Does Wrong.** Skeleton. Most platforms expand network size without expanding receiver budget, so they push everything toward the complexity floor. Engagement optimization, recommendation systems, algorithmic feeds. The specific mechanisms by which the complexity ceiling gets forced lower.

**Chapter 10: Infrastructure For Complexity.** Skeleton. What would tech look like if it tried to preserve complexity across networks rather than compressing it? What's the modern equivalent of Harari's bureaucracy: the apparatus that holds complex truth across networks and lifespans?

## Open questions

- **Function vs. statistical regularity.** Is complexity/virality a real function, or a strong tendency? Currently treating as a strong tendency, but the function version would let you measure complexity ceilings of given networks.
- **Harari's truth/order axis vs. complexity/virality.** Probably entangled but distinct. Whether this gets its own chapter or stays as an appendix depends on how much weight it carries.
- **Wikipedia, long-form podcasts, religions over centuries.** Counter-examples that mostly reinforce the institutional-carriers refinement but deserve fuller treatment somewhere.
- **Is the prescription technological or social?** Maybe the way through is cultural and tech is just supporting infrastructure. Honestly don't know yet.
- **Three-realities framework.** Where does it best live? Leaning Chapter 1 or 2 when defining what "information" even is.
